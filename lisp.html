General History

Lisp is a functional programming language designed for easy manipulation of data strings. It's the second-oldest high-level programming language in the world. It was invented by John McCarthy in 1958 at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. Lisp stands for list processing and was originally created as a practical mathematical notation for computer programs. Today, Lisp is popular in artificial intelligence research. Lisp introduced tree data structures, automatic storage management, dynamic typing, and self-hosting compilers which are now widely used ideas in computer science.
